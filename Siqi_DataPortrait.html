6 – Data Portrait

My Mood in a Week

This data portrait shows my mood changes during one week through colors and small moving mood dots, because I found my mood will change each day, and I usually use different colors to represent my mood.

How to read my data portrait:
The background shows the emotional temperature from warm colors (bottom) to cold colors (top). Each white circle at the bottom stands for each day of the week. When you move the mouse, the dots gently move up and down or rise, reflecting my emotional rhythm across the week.

Design process:
I tracked my mood for seven days and gave each day a color based on how I felt. I first sketched color layers like a gradient of emotions, then created moving mood dots in p5.js. The motion and interactivity were inspired by Dear Data and how emotions are never static; they always change. By visualizing it, I could “see” my emotions instead of only feeling them.
